Paul Pogba: A Visual Essay Of His Time At Manchester United

This visual essay will explore the French midfielder Paul Pogba during his time at Manchester United.

We will first discuss what kind of player he is, his position and how he plays. Then we will showcase his strengths and weaknesses. Finally, we will compare his and this teammates' performances to understand his importance to the team.

What kind of player is Paul Pogba?

Hover over any pass/circle (where applicable) to highlight the entire group.

The Show Pass Clusters chart shows the clusters where Pogba starts his passes from. Any number of clusters shows that Pogba is a box-to-box midfielder.

For 5 or more clusters, a pattern emerges which shows that the largest clusters come from the right half-space (the highlighted area on the pitch) which makes sense as the majority of the time, Pogba played as the right defensive midfielder alongside Matić during that season. Generally speaking, the clusters get smaller the further away from the right side of the pitch.

When the number of clusters is 10 and % of passes shown is 100%, the same as the number of outfield players in a match, interesting formations like 3-4-3, 3-3-4, and 4-3-3 emerge. The chart shows Pogba gets involved all over the pitch, helping the defenders pass out from the back, starting attacks from the defensive midfield position, and creating chances from outside the opponent's box. The left-wing cluster also has more passes than the right-wing cluster due to Pogba playing a few games in that position.

The Show Shot Map chart shows Pogba likes to shoot mostly from the left half-space but isn't very accurate. It also shows he's not afraid to get into the box and score.

The Show Assist/Key Pass Map chart shows that Pogba mainly creates chances and assists from deep in midfield but occasionally gets into the box and wings to create.

What are his strengths and weaknesses?

Strengths

The Show Through Balls / Match chart again shows Pogba like to make through passes from deep for his attackers to run onto. The Show Duels / Match chart shows Pogba is one the best in the Premier League at competing with his opponent to win the ball in a duel. The Show Successful 50/50s / Match chart shows he's one the best at 50/50 challenges. The Show Assists / Match chart shows he's one highest assisting players in the league which is surprising as he typically plays less forward than the other top players. The Show Passes / Match shows Pogba likes to get on the ball, make things happen and control the game. The Show Goals / Match shows Pogba ranks similarly to top attackers like K. Bruyne, D. Jota, and D. Calvert-Lewin for goals despite playing mainly as a defensive midfielder.

Weaknesses

The Show Big Chances Created / Match chart shows Pogba ranks 46th meaning he doesn't create chances at the same rate as the top players despite being one of the top assisting players. The Show Recoveries / Match chart shows Pogba ranks 19th meaning he doesn't have quite the defensive work rate as other defensive midfielders. The Show Interceptions / Match chart shows Pogba is one of the worst in the league ranking at 163rd again showing his defensive work rate doesn't match other defensive midfielders like W. Ndidi and N. Kanté. The Show Tackles / Match chart tells the same story.

How does Pogba compare to his teammates?

Hover over any line/circle to highlight the entire group.

The PlayeRank framework scores players on how well they played in a particular match. The Show Midfielders chart shows that Pogba generally outperforms his midfield teammates on a game-by-game basis. The Show Attackers chart shows that Pogba performs similarly to his attacking teammates.

Conclusion

This essay has shown that Pogba is a box-to-box midfielder that likes to go all over the pitch but mainly operates in the right half-space to pass, make things happen, and control the game.

Pogba excels at using his physical abilities to win duels and 50/50 challenges. His goal and assist output rival the best attackers in the league despite playing deeper. Where Pogba can improve is the defensive side of the game. However, after looking at his strengths and weaknesses, it's clear that the numbers point to Pogba being better utilised as an attacking midfielder that doesn't have to worry as much about defence.

Finally, Pogba is one the best-performing players in his team when compared to the attackers and defenders.
